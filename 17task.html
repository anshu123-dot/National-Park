About Us

Jim Corbett National Park is one of India's oldest and most prestigious national parks, located in the state of Uttarakhand. Established in 1936 as Hailey National Park, it was later renamed in honor of Jim Corbett, a renowned British-Indian hunter, conservationist, and author, who played a key role in its establishment. Here are some key points about Jim Corbett National Park:
1.Location: It is situated in the foothills of the Himalayas, covering an area of approximately 520 square kilometers (200 square miles). The park extends over the districts of Nainital, Almora, and Pauri Garhwal in the state of Uttarakhand, India.
2.Biodiversity: Jim Corbett National Park is renowned for its rich biodiversity. It is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including Bengal tigers, elephants, leopards, deer, wild boars, and various species of birds and reptiles. The park's ecosystem consists of dense forests, grasslands, rivers, and hills, providing a habitat for a wide variety of wildlife.
3.Tiger Reserve: It is one of the first tiger reserves in India and is part of the larger Corbett Tiger Reserve, established under Project Tiger to protect the endangered Bengal tiger. The park's tiger population is one of the main attractions for wildlife enthusiasts and tourists.
4.Activities: Visitors to Jim Corbett National Park can enjoy a range of activities, including jeep safaris, elephant safaris, birdwatching, nature walks, and wildlife photography. The park offers opportunities for both adventure and leisure, allowing visitors to explore its natural beauty and observe wildlife in their natural habitat.
5.Conservation: Conservation efforts are a key focus of Jim Corbett National Park. It plays a crucial role in preserving India's natural heritage and protecting endangered species, particularly the Bengal tiger. The park is actively involved in wildlife conservation, habitat management, and community engagement initiatives to promote sustainable tourism and environmental stewardship.
6.Accessibility: Jim Corbett National Park is easily accessible from major cities like Delhi, Dehradun, and Lucknow. The nearest airport is at Pantnagar, approximately 50 kilometers away, while the nearest railway station is at Ramnagar, which is well-connected to various cities in India.
